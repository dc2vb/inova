A Connected Patient Intelligence Ecosystem

My initial vision is to build a unified, real-time patient intelligence hub that connects fragmented data across clinical, digital, and operational channels into a single, actionable view. Powered by CDP and CJA (and assuming Adobe Analytics), this ecosystem will allow Inova to understand the full context of each patient’s journey across devices, channels, and stages of care and engagement.

My goal is to establish a powerful, real-time patient intelligence hub. This hub will empower us to deliver highly personalized, empathetic, and effective patient experiences, optimize marketing investments, and drive measurable growth across all Inova Health service lines. We'll achieve this by leveraging AEP's incredible capabilities to create a single, holistic view of each patient, analyze their complex healthcare journeys, and activate personalized engagements at a scale we've never seen before. Imagine the possibilities when we truly understand and anticipate our patients' needs!

Phased Implementation Plan (12-Week Agile)

This agile AEP implementation is structured into five condensed phases, designed for rapid foundational setup and pilot deployment within a 12-week timeframe. Each phase has specific objectives, key activities, and deliverables. Below is a visual timeline overview of these accelerated phases, including a dedicated "soft launch" period.

Project Timeline Overview (12-Week Agile Implementation)

This timeline represents an accelerated, foundational implementation focusing on a critical pilot use case.

Phase 1: Discovery & Core Requirements (Weeks 1-2)

Objective: Rapidly define critical business objectives for a pilot AEP implementation, identify key stakeholders, and outline essential data sources within a compressed timeframe.

Key Activities:

  • 1.1 High-Level Stakeholder Alignment: Conduct focused workshops to align on a single, high-impact pilot use case for AEP.
  • 1.2 Core Data Source Identification: Identify the minimum viable data sources required for the pilot.
  • 1.3 Critical KPI & Success Definition: Define 2-3 key metrics for the pilot's success.

Deliverables:

Pilot Use Case Definition & Scope Document
Identified Core Data Sources for Pilot
Pilot KPIs & Success Metrics

Phase 2: Architecture & Design (Weeks 3-5)

Figure 3: Illustrative screenshot of AEP's capability to integrate with CRM systems for unified patient profiles.

Objective: Design an extensible, privacy-resilient, and healthcare-compliant data architecture that supports identity resolution, real-time activation, and journey-level analysis.

Key Activities:

  • 2.1 AEP Solution Architecture Design: Develop the end-to-end data flow model, including ingestion pathways for both batch and streaming sources. Define how behavioral, clinical, and engagement data will be structured and made available for segmentation, orchestration, and analytics. Establish the foundation for scalable patient profile creation and cross-channel visibility.
  • 2.2 Experience Data Model (XDM) Design: Define healthcare-specific schemas that represent critical entities such as patients, encounters, service lines, digital behaviors, and communication preferences. Ensure data is normalized to support consistent attribution, journey tracking, and activation logic across all patient engagement touchpoints.
  • 2.3 Identity Strategy & Resolution Rules: Design framework to unify patient records across known (e.g., CRM, patient portal logins) and unknown (e.g., anonymous site behavior) identifiers. Configure merging logic and identity priority rules to support accurate audience creation and personalization while preserving privacy and HIPAA compliance.
  • 2.4 Journey Workspace & Reporting Design: Design intuitive, role-based reporting workspaces that provide stakeholders visibility into patient journey performance, conversion paths, and engagement trends. Define core metrics and dimensions that reflect both clinical and digital KPIs (e.g., appointment completions, repeat visit frequency, digital channel influence).
  • 2.5 Audience Design & Activation Framework: Develop audience definitions and segmentation logic to support initial use cases—such as patient reactivation, new patient acquisition, or service-line-specific outreach. Establish workflows to push these audiences into activation platforms (e.g., email, web personalization, contact center), with clear governance rules for campaign orchestration.
  • 2.6 Integration Blueprint: Create a detailed integration plan to connect AEP with key martech systems. Include specs for tagging, consent capture, and measurement enablement across digital assets.

Deliverables:

AEP Architecture Blueprint & Data Flow Diagrams
Experience Data Model (XDM) Schemas
Identity Strategy Documentation
Journey Reporting Workspace Specs
Segmentation Framework & Use Case Mapping
Martech Integration Plan

Phase 3: Implementation & Data Ingestion (Weeks 6-8)

Figure 4: Example visualization of Adobe Analytics data being ingested and utilized within AEP for comprehensive insights.

Objective: Deploy and configure Adobe Experience Platform to begin unifying patient data, establishing real-time profiles, and surfacing actionable insights—while validating data integrity, journey logic, and system interoperability.

Key Activities:

  • 3.1 Environment Provisioning & Configuration: Stand up AEP instances (production and development sandboxes) with defined governance protocols, including naming conventions, workspace access, and role-based permissions. Enable core AEP services required for journey analysis, profile enrichment, and activation.
  • 3.2 Data Ingestion Pipeline Development: Implement scalable connectors and ETL pipelines to ingest priority data sources—starting with CRM, web analytics, email engagement, and patient portal behavior. Normalize ingested data to match Experience Data Model (XDM) standards and ensure tagging aligns with downstream use cases.
  • 3.3 Identity Graph Configuration: Enable and tune the identity graph to resolve known and pseudonymous identifiers, allowing for profile stitching across channels. Configure merging policies to respect consent, regulatory flags, and healthcare-specific ID hierarchies (e.g., MRNs, hashed emails, device IDs).
  • 3.4 Profile Assembly & Real-Time Evaluation: Begin building unified patient profiles using streaming and batch data. Establish evaluation logic for real-time profile updates, and test initial activation scenarios (e.g., triggering a follow-up email after a form submission or site action). Monitor system latency and data freshness benchmarks.
  • 3.5 Journey Analytics Workspace Buildout: Configure workspace dashboards to monitor early patient behavior signals, conversion trends, and digital engagement performance. Define filters and segments tied to initial pilot use cases (e.g., new patient appointments, repeat visitors, content engagement by service line).
  • 3.6 Pilot Use Case Implementation: Activate a low-risk, high-value pilot such as personalized content delivery for new patients or remarketing to those who abandoned an appointment booking flow. Use this pilot to validate identity resolution, segmentation, activation, and reporting fidelity end to end.

Deliverables:

Configured AEP Environment (Prod + Dev)
Data Ingestion Pipelines for Priority Sources
Operational Identity Graph
Unified Patient Profiles with Evaluation Rules
Initial Journey Analytics Dashboards
Live Pilot Use Case with Activation + Reporting Validation

Phase 4: Testing, Validation & Optimization (Weeks 9-10)

Objective: Ensure the integrity, reliability, and performance of Adobe Experience Platform implementation—validating that data flows, profile assembly, activation logic, and reporting align with stakeholder expectations and regulatory standards.

Key Activities:

  • 4.1 Data Quality Assurance & Reconciliation: Conduct rigorous data validation across all ingested sources to confirm accuracy, completeness, and mapping to the Experience Data Model (XDM). Reconcile sample records from source systems (e.g., CRM, EHR, analytics) against unified profiles to identify merge errors, missing fields, or inconsistent formatting. Document data lineage for transparency.
  • 4.2 Profile Resolution & Segment Accuracy Audits: Test identity stitching across devices, channels, and data sources to ensure profiles resolve correctly based on configured rules. Evaluate the precision of key audience segments (e.g., prospective patients, frequent service-line visitors) to confirm they align with business definitions and consent logic.
  • 4.3 User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Facilitate role-based testing with marketing, analytics, and IT users across dashboards, segmentation workflows, and activation outputs. Capture feedback on usability, naming conventions, access levels, and data interpretation to fine-tune the platform before wider rollout.
  • 4.4 Performance & Scalability Testing: Evaluate AEP’s responsiveness under simulated peak loads, including real-time data ingestion and segment evaluation. Measure latency benchmarks for data availability, activation triggers, and reporting refresh intervals. Ensure infrastructure can scale with anticipated volume increases across additional use cases.
  • 4.5 Security, Privacy & Compliance Audit: Review all workflows involving patient data to verify compliance with HIPAA, Inova policies, and industry security standards. Confirm access control policies are functioning as expected and that consent preferences are respected in audience-building and activation scenarios.
  • 4.6 Optimization & Refinement: Based on testing results and stakeholder input, refine identity merge rules, XDM schemas, reporting visualizations, and tagging logic. Resolve known issues before expansion and prepare documentation for post-go-live support and future enhancements.

Deliverables:

Data Quality Validation & Reconciliation Report
Segment & Profile Accuracy Audit
User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Log & Resolution Plan
Platform Performance Benchmark Report
Security & Privacy Compliance Checklist
Final Optimized Configuration (Pre-Go-Live)

Phase 5: Deployment & Enablement (Weeks 11-12)

Objective: Transition Adobe Experience Platform from pilot to production by rolling out validated use cases, enabling cross-functional teams, and establishing operational support systems to ensure sustained value and platform adoption across Inova Health.

Key Activities:

  • 5.1 Phased Rollout Execution: Initiate deployment with the validated pilot use case, ensuring activation and reporting components remain stable in a live environment. Gradually scale to additional audiences, service lines, and touchpoints, prioritizing low-risk, high-impact opportunities (e.g., personalized follow-up messaging, digital remarketing, or high-volume appointment flows).
  • 5.2 Stakeholder Training & Documentation: Deliver structured enablement sessions tailored to platform roles—marketers, analysts, campaign managers, and IT partners. Include hands-on walkthroughs for audience creation, reporting interpretation, data layer hygiene, and activation workflows. Provide quick-reference guides, governance playbooks, and dashboard overviews to support adoption and self-service.
  • 5.3 Operational Ownership & Support Model: Define platform ownership responsibilities across key functions (e.g., marketing operations, analytics, IT, data governance). Establish triage processes for platform bugs, data quality issues, and use case requests. Develop a runbook and support framework that aligns with Inova’s enterprise change management and digital operations practices.
  • 5.4 Change Management & Adoption Planning: Partner with internal champions to build awareness around the platform’s impact and ongoing roadmap. Launch internal communications to showcase early wins and benefits realized (e.g., campaign efficiency gains, engagement lift). Address cultural or operational resistance with proactive training and proof-of-value storytelling.

Deliverables:

Phased Use Case Deployment Plan
Role-Specific Training Sessions & Enablement Materials
Platform Support Model & Ownership Framework
Governance Runbook & Issue Resolution Process
Change Management & Adoption Strategy

Continuous Optimization & Innovation (Ongoing)

Objective: Maximize long-term value from Adobe Experience Platform by establishing a framework for continuous optimization, advanced use case expansion, and innovation across Inova’s market intelligence and patient engagement efforts.

Key Activities:

  • 6.1 Performance Monitoring & Measurement: Establish ongoing reporting cadences and executive dashboards to monitor core KPIs tied to AEP use cases—e.g., campaign ROI, engagement lift, audience reach, and appointment conversion trends. Continuously evaluate platform performance (data freshness, identity resolution rates, activation reliability) to ensure scalability and efficiency.
  • 6.2 New Use Case Development & Expansion: Identify new cross-functional use cases that can leverage AEP—such as service-line-specific outreach, care journey reminders, or personalization for long-term condition management. Collaborate with business owners to prioritize and roadmap new journeys, segments, and channels aligned to enterprise goals.
  • 6.3 Feature Adoption & Platform Maturity: Track releases and capabilities added to Adobe’s product roadmap and assess relevance to Inova’s objectives. Pilot new features such as real-time decisioning, propensity scoring, or more granular consent management. Maintain alignment with Adobe’s platform evolution to unlock additional value over time.
  • 6.4 Advanced Analytics & Predictive Modeling: Integrate advanced analytics capabilities (e.g., machine learning models, predictive segmentation, or health risk indicators) into the AEP framework. Collaborate with data science teams to enrich profiles with calculated insights that support proactive, personalized outreach strategies.
  • 6.5 Governance Reviews & Feedback Loops: Host recurring stakeholder reviews to report performance, gather feedback, and assess alignment with changing business priorities. Maintain a backlog of enhancement requests, data needs, and use case ideas. Continuously improve schema, identity rules, and audience logic based on real-world usage and evolving regulations.
